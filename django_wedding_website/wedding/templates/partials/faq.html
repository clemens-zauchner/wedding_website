How to get to Innsbruck

A flight to Innsbruck directly is very beautiful as you will see a lot of mountains but it can be quite expensive. If you fly in to Innsbruck directly, there is a public bus (F) to connect you to the city centre.

There is also the option to fly to Munich and to take the train or depending on when you will arrive, an airport taxi. We can recommend fourseasons. Be careful not to book a flight to Munich West/Memmingen. There are no trains there.

There is a direct OEBB Railjet train to Innsbruck from Vienna Airport, but the train journey is around 4.5 hours.

From Zurich Airport you can take a short train ride to Zurich train station to get on the OEBB Railjet to Innsbruck. The journey is about 4 hours.

Getting around in Innsbruck

Public transport

It is very easy to get around Innsbruck. In most cases you don't need public transport, as everything is within a 10-15 minute walking distance. However, there are trams and busses available. There are daily and weekly tickets available. An overview of tickets can be found at IVB for Innsbruck and at VVT for all of Tyrol.

Taxi

Unfortunately there is no Uber in Innsbruck. You can call a taxi by dialling +43 512 5311 or +43 512 206000

How to get to the church?

There is a public bus directly from the city center of Innsbruck to the church. It's line J. With the car it's a 15 min drive. There is free car parking at Congresspark.
